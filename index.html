With A Million Students Back In Classrooms, This Is How New York City Schools Plan To Combat Covid-19 This Fall

The above map, provided by the New York City Department of Education, shows how the virus has affected public schools since the start of the pandemic. Red dots show schools that went fully remote last year. Blue dots show schools where classrooms closed. Orange dots show a school where classrooms have partially quarantined. Purple dots show a school where a staff member had to quarantine but students didn’t.


By Gina Heeb

Mayor Bill de Blasio declined to restore a full-time remote option at New York City public schools this fall, arguing that it would hamper the quality of education and cut students off from support systems. As the public school system welcomed back its nearly one million students this month, de Blasio has said that vaccinations, mask requirements, coronavirus tests and other measures would allow students and staff to attend classes in person. Here’s what you need to know about how the 1,800 public schools in the city plan to combat Covid-19 in classrooms.
